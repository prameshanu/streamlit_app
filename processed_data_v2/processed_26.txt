art and architecture in the golden age of pericles art and architecture flourished during the golden age of pericles a the athenian sought to demonstrate their wealth power and cultural sophistication one of the most iconic architectural achievement of this period wa the construction of the parthenon a temple dedicated to the goddess athena designed by the renowned architect ictinus and callicrates and overseen by the sculptor phidias the parthenon is considered the epitome of classical greek architecture it harmonious proportion intricate sculpture and stunning marble frieze depicted mythological scene showcasing the mastery of ancient greek artist in addition to the parthenon the golden age witnessed the construction of several other notable structure such a the propylaea the erechtheion and the temple of hephaestus these building showcased the innovative architectural technique developed during this period including the use of the doric ionic and corinthian order the athenian also adorned their city with numerous sculpture such a the famous bronze statue of athena promachos and the marble statue of the river god ilissos both created by phidias these sculpture not only embodied the ideal of beauty and perfection but also conveyed important cultural and political message
