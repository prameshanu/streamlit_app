hellenistic period the influence of greek culture the hellenistic period which spanned from the death of alexander the great in 323 bce to the roman conquest of egypt in 30 bce marked a significant chapter in ancient greek history during this time the vast empire established by alexander the great not only experienced political and territorial change but also witnessed the spread and influence of greek culture throughout the region it encompassed this chapter will delve into the various aspect of greek culture that left an indelible impact on the hellenistic world one of the most evident influence of greek culture during the hellenistic period wa the spread of the greek language known a koine greek alexander the great conquest facilitated the dissemination of this language across his vast empire extending from greece to egypt and a far east a india koine greek became the lingua franca of the hellenistic world allowing for communication and cultural exchange between diverse population this language served a a unifying force promoting a sense of greek identity and facilitating the spread of greek idea literature and philosophy another notable aspect of greek culture that permeated the hellenistic world wa the spread of greek art and architecture the hellenistic period witnessed the development of new artistic style and technique blending element from different culture within the empire the influence of greek sculpture can be seen in the realistic portrayal of human emotion and the increased emphasis on individualism monumental building and grand city constructed during this period also showcased greek architectural element such a the use of column pediment and symmetrical design the famous library of alexandria founded during the hellenistic period became a hub for intellectual pursuit and housed countless greek text further disseminating greek knowledge and culture
