the archaic period and the birth of city state around 800 bce greece emerged from the dark age entering a new era known a the archaic period this period marked the rebirth of greek civilization a it witnessed significant cultural political and social change one of the most defining feature of the archaic period wa the establishment of city state or polis which became the backbone of ancient greece these city state were independent entity with their own government law and identity athens sparta and corinth were among the most prominent and influential city state of this time the rise of city state led to increased trade and interaction among greek colonization became a common practice a city state sent out settler to establish new colony in distant land this expansion not only allowed the greek to spread their influence but also provided economic opportunity and alleviated population pressure the archaic period also witnessed significant advancement in art literature and philosophy the epic poem of homer such a the iliad and the odyssey were composed during this time shaping greek literature and serving a a testament to the cultural richness of the period in conclusion the birth of ancient greece can be traced back to the minoan and mycenaeans who laid the foundation of civilization in the aegean region the collapse of the mycenaean civilization plunged greece into a dark age but from this period of decline emerged a rejuvenated greek society the archaic period saw the birth of city state the spread of greek culture and the flourishing of art and literature these pivotal development set the stage for the golden age of greece a period of unparalleled intellectual and artistic achievement that would shape the course of western civilization
