the rise of sparta in stark contrast to athens sparta wa a militaristic city state that valued discipline strength and military prowess above all else located in the southern part of greece sparta wa known for it rigorous military training and highly disciplined society the spartan society wa built on the principle of producing strong warrior and from a young age boy were subjected to a strict training regimen known a the agoge spartan society wa divided into three distinct class spartiates perioikoi and helot the spartiates were the ruling class and consisted of full spartan citizen who underwent the agoge the perioikoi were free inhabitant of the surrounding territory who enjoyed limited right and served a craftsman and merchant the helot were the lowest class and were essentially state owned slave who worked the land the spartan political system wa a unique blend of monarchy oligarchy and democracy the two king served a the head of the state but their power were limited by a council of elder known a the gerousia additionally there wa an assembly of all spartan adult male citizen known a the apella which had the power to approve or reject proposal made by the gerousia in conclusion the rise of city state in ancient greece marked a significant turning point in history athens and sparta with their diverse political system and contrasting ideology emerged a two of the most influential city state of the time while athens thrived a a democratic and intellectual center sparta excelled in military strength and discipline the legacy of these city state continue to shape our understanding of ancient greece and it enduring impact on western civilization
