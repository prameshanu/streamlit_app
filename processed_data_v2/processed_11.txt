the rise of athens athens often hailed a the birthplace of democracy wa a remarkable city state that flourished during the golden age of greece the athenian democracy wa established in the 5th century bce making it one of the earliest known democratic system in the world the democratic reform were initiated by solon in the 6th century bce but it wa pericles who truly solidified the democratic principle and led athens to it pinnacle of power and influence under pericles leadership athens experienced a period of great prosperity artistic achievement and intellectual advancement known a the periclean age this period saw the construction of magnificent architectural wonder such a the parthenon which still stand a an enduring symbol of ancient greece athens became an epicenter of knowledge attracting philosopher writer and artist from all over the mediterranean the athenian democracy wa characterized by direct participation of citizen in the decision making process all male citizen above the age of 18 had the right to vote and participate in the assembly where important decision regarding legislation and foreign policy were made however it is essential to note that not all individual were considered citizen woman slave and foreigner were excluded from political participation
