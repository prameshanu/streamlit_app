science and mathematics in ancient greece the beginning of greek science and mathematics ancient greece is often hailed a the birthplace of western civilization and for good reason the ancient greek made remarkable contribution to various field including science and mathematics this chapter explores the pioneering work of greek thinker in these discipline shedding light on their innovative idea and groundbreaking discovery the origin of greek science and mathematics can be traced back to the seventh century bce at this time the greek transitioned from a mythological worldview to a more rational and empirical approach this shift in thinking laid the foundation for scientific inquiry and mathematical exploration one of the earliest greek mathematician wa thales of miletus he is credited with introducing deductive reasoning and geometric principle to solve practical problem thales work paved the way for future mathematician to delve deeper into the realm of number and shape another influential figure wa pythagoras who explored the relationship between mathematics and music the pythagorean theorem which state that in a right angled triangle the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the square of the other two side is a testament to pythagoras mathematical genius greek science also flourished during this period the natural philosopher anaximander is considered one of the first to propose a scientific explanation for the origin of the universe he posited that the universe arose from a boundless eternal substance known a the apeiron anaximander cosmological idea laid the groundwork for future theory on the nature of the cosmos a greek city state grew in power and influence so did their commitment to scientific and mathematical pursuit athens in particular became a center for intellectual and scientific endeavor the athenian philosopher democritus developed the concept of atomism suggesting that all matter is composed of tiny indivisible particle called atom while his theory were not fully accepted during his lifetime democritus idea foreshadowed the modern understanding of atomic structure
