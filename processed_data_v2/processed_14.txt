the political landscape of ancient greece while athens wa renowned for it democratic system other city state in ancient greece had different form of government sparta for example had a unique political structure that combined element of monarchy oligarchy and militarism spartan society wa governed by two king who shared power and were responsible for leading the military below the king the gerousia a council of elderly and experienced men held significant authority this council proposed legislation supervised the king and acted a a judicial body the gerousia consisted of 30 member who were selected for life in addition to the gerousia sparta had an assembly known a the apella where citizen could voice their opinion but had limited decision making power only male citizen over the age of 30 were allowed to participate excluding a significant portion of the population sparta political system focused primarily on maintaining a strong military and preserving social order another city state corinth implemented a system of government that combined feature of both oligarchy and democracy a council of wealthy citizen known a the oligarchy held the highest decision making authority however this council wa subject to the approval of the assembly where all male citizen could participate this hybrid political structure allowed for a degree of popular participation while still preserving the influence of the elite
