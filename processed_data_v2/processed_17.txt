democracy in athens at the heart of athens legacy lie it pioneering experiment with democracy unlike any other city state at the time athens established a system of government that allowed it citizen to participate directly in decision making this radical concept known a demokratia laid the foundation for modern democratic principle the birth of athenian democracy can be traced back to the reform of cleisthenes in the late 6th century bce cleisthenes introduced the idea of equal participation for all eligible citizen regardless of their social status this marked a significant departure from the traditional oligarchic rule prevalent in ancient greece in the athenian democracy citizen had the right to vote speak freely in public assembly and even hold public office one of the most important institution of athenian democracy wa the assembly known a the ecclesia all male citizen over the age of 18 were eligible to attend the assembly where they could debate and vote on various issue including legislation foreign policy and the appointment of official this inclusive system gave every citizen a voice ensuring that power wa not concentrated in the hand of a few privileged individual another significant aspect of athenian democracy wa the use of sortition a process of selecting public official by lot rather than through election this practice aimed to prevent corruption and the influence of wealth in politics it also allowed for a more diverse representation of citizen ensuring that anyone could hold public office
