plato and the theory of form plato a student of socrates and one of the most influential philosopher in history carried forward his mentor legacy and developed his own philosophical system born in athens around 428 bce plato founded the academy an institution that played a crucial role in the development of greek thought at the core of plato philosophy is the theory of form according to plato the physical world we perceive through our sens is merely a flawed reflection of a higher realm of perfect and unchanging form these form such a justice beauty and truth exist independently of the material world and serve a the ultimate reality the physical world with it imperfection and transience is merely a shadow of these eternal form plato believed that true knowledge could only be attained through reason and intellectual contemplation of the form he argued that philosopher who posse a unique capacity for insight should rule society a they have a deep understanding of the form and can guide others towards a just and harmonious existence plato philosophical idea extended beyond metaphysics he explored topic such a ethic politics and epistemology leaving a lasting impact on numerous field of study his famous dialogue including the republic and the symposium continue to be studied and debated by scholar and philosopher to this day
