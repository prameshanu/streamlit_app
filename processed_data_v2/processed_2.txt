the collapse and dark age around 1200 bce the mycenaean civilization faced a sudden and catastrophic collapse the reason for this decline remain a subject of debate among historian with theory ranging from natural disaster to internal strife and external invasion whatever the cause the fall of the mycenaeans plunged the aegean region into a period of darkness and decline this era known a the dark age lasted approximately four century it wa characterized by a decline in population the abandonment of city and the loss of writing and literacy the once thriving trade network disintegrated and greece entered a period of isolation during this time the greek experienced significant cultural and societal change the population shifted from urban center to rural settlement leading to the rise of independent farming community the economy became more self sufficient relying on agriculture and local trade despite the challenge of the dark age it is during this period that the seed of the greek renaissance were planted the isolated community began to develop their unique cultural identity which would later become the foundation of ancient greece it wa also during this time that the oral tradition of storytelling thrived passing down myth legend and historical account from one generation to the next
