the great persian invasion following darius is death in 486 bce his son xerxes i ascended the persian throne and inherited his father ambition to conquer greece xerxes fueled by a desire for revenge and to assert persia dominance assembled an enormous army and fleet to launch a second invasion of greece in 480 bce the persian force consisting of hundred of thousand of soldier embarked on a perilous journey across the hellespont a narrow strait separating asia from europe xerxes army moved through thrace and macedonia leaving a trail of destruction in it wake the persian navy accompanying the land force sailed parallel to their advance the greek city state aware of the impending threat put aside their difference and formed an alliance known a the hellenic league led by athens and sparta the greek sought to defend their homeland against the persian invader the hellenic league devised a strategy to block the persian advance at the narrow pas of thermopylae and engage the persian navy at the strait of artemisium at the battle of thermopylae a small force of greek soldier including the famous spartan warrior held off the much larger persian army for several day however a traitor revealed a mountain path that allowed the persian to flank the greek position leading to their defeat despite this setback the greek resistance had bought valuable time simultaneously the greek navy engaged the persian fleet at the battle of artemisium although the greek suffered heavy loss they managed to inflict significant damage on the persian navy preventing them from achieving total control over the sea
