the birth of ancient greece the origin of ancient greece ancient greece a land steeped in history and myth cultivated some of the most significant contribution to western civilization to understand the birth of ancient greece we must delve into it origin tracing back to the bronze age and the development of early civilization in the aegean region the story begin around 3000 bce when a group of seafaring people known a the minoan settled on the island of crete the minoan were an advanced society boasting impressive architecture intricate artwork and a thriving trade network their influence extended across the aegean fostering contact with neighboring culture such a the egyptian and mesopotamians crete served a a cultural hub but it wa not the only significant region in the birth of ancient greece on the mainland a civilization known a the mycenaeans emerged around 1600 bce influenced by the minoan the mycenaeans adopted their writing system religious practice and artistic style they constructed fortified city and engaged in extensive trade bringing wealth and prosperity to the region the mycenaeans played a pivotal role in shaping ancient greece their military prowess reflected in the famous city of mycenae allowed them to dominate the region they expanded their influence to other part of the aegean establishing colony and controlling trade route however this period of dominance would not last forever
