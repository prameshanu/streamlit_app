democracy in ancient greece democracy a system of government in which power is vested in the people originated in ancient greece the birthplace of democracy athens led the way in establishing a political system that allowed citizen to participate in decision making process this chapter explores the development of democracy and the political landscape of ancient greece the concept of democracy emerged in athens during the 5th century bce a period known a the golden age of greece this era witnessed an unprecedented flourishing of art culture and intellectual pursuit it wa in this context that athenian democracy took root providing citizen with a platform to voice their opinion and influence policy at the heart of athenian democracy wa the assembly known a the ecclesia which consisted of all male citizen over the age of 18 they met regularly to discus and vote on important matter such a legislation declaration of war and the appointment of official this direct participation of citizen in decision making wa a revolutionary departure from the prevailing system of monarchy and oligarchy the assembly wa not without it limitation however the sheer size of athens population made it difficult for all citizen to attend meeting regularly moreover only male citizen were eligible to participate excluding woman slave and foreigner nevertheless athenian democracy marked a significant step towards the inclusion of citizen in the political process laying the groundwork for future democratic system another important institution in ancient greek democracy wa the council of 500 known a the boule comprised of 500 citizen selected by lot the boule served a the executive body of athens it prepared the agenda for the assembly proposed legislation and managed day to day affair of the city state it member served for one year and were chosen from each of the ten tribe that made up athens the practice of selecting official by lot rather than through election wa a distinctive feature of ancient greek democracy this ensured a more equitable distribution of power and prevented the concentration of authority in the hand of a few elite individual furthermore position in the government were open to all citizen regardless of wealth or social status emphasizing the principle of political equality
