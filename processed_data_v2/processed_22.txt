the persian war greece v the mighty empire the prelude to the persian war in the 5th century bce the ancient world witnessed a series of conflict that would shape the destiny of greece and leave a lasting impact on the course of history these conflict known a the persian war pitted the mighty persian empire against the city state of greece the battle that ensued were not only significant military encounter but also symbolized the struggle between two vastly different civilization the dominant persian empire and the fiercely independent and democratic greek city state the persian war were a culmination of century of tension between greece and persia the vast persian empire under the rule of darius i and later xerxes i sought to expand it dominion by conquering the greek city state persia with it immense military and economic resource aimed to bring these fiercely independent state under it control however the greek with their deep love for freedom and democratic ideal were determined to resist persian aggression the conflict began in 499 bce when several greek city state in asia minor then under persian rule rebelled against persian domination athens and eretria two prominent greek city state supported this rebellion leading to a swift persian retaliation while initially unsuccessful the persian response set the stage for what would become a series of major conflict between persia and greece in 490 bce the persian empire under the command of darius i launched a full scale invasion of greece the persian force set their sight on athens hoping to subdue the greek resistance however the greek led by the athenian achieved a stunning victory at the battle of marathon this triumph not only halted the persian advance but also showcased the military prowess of the greek city state
