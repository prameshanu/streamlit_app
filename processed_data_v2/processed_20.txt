social structure and upbringing sparta social structure wa unlike any other in ancient greece it wa strictly divided into three class the spartiates the perioikoi and the helot at the top of the hierarchy were the spartiates who were full citizen and served a the backbone of the spartan military they were descendant of the original dorian conqueror and were required to undergo rigorous military training from a young age the upbringing of spartan male wa centered around physical and military education at the age of seven spartan boy were taken from their family and enrolled in the agoge a state run educational system here they were taught discipline endurance and the art of warfare they were subjected to a harsh regime that included physical training harsh living condition and constant testing of their mental and physical ability this rigorous training aimed to produce capable and fearless warrior the spartan way of life emphasized selflessness and obedience to the state boy were encouraged to develop strong bond among themselves forming tight knit unit known a syssitia these unit fostered camaraderie and loyalty essential quality in battle spartan girl also received physical education to ensure they bore strong and healthy offspring for the spartan state this focus on physical training and discipline played a significant role in shaping the warrior society of sparta
