major event and strategy the peloponnesian war can be divided into three distinct phase the first phase known a the archidamian war lasted from 431 to 421 bce it began with a spartan invasion of attica the region surrounding athens aiming to weaken the athenian economy and morale however athenian guided by pericles strategy of relying on their powerful navy and the safety of their fortified city wall endured the spartan invasion the war in this phase largely revolved around skirmish siege and occasional battle the second phase the sicilian expedition took place from 415 to 413 bce athens seeking to expand it influence and weaken sparta launched an ambitious campaign to conquer syracuse a powerful city state in sicily however this expedition turned into a disaster for athens a their large fleet and army were ultimately defeated leading to a significant loss of resource and a decline in athenian power the third phase known a the ionian war lasted from 413 to 404 bce in this phase sparta with the support of persia launched a series of successful campaign against athens and it remaining ally athens faced several naval defeat most notably the battle of aegospotami where the spartan fleet under lysander decisively defeated the athenian navy effectively cutting off athens from it vital grain supply route this victory led to the final siege of athens resulting in it surrender in 404 bce throughout the war both athens and sparta employed different strategy athens relied on it navy using it superior fleet to control the sea and access resource from it overseas empire this strategy aimed to wear down the spartan over time a they were primarily a land based power sparta on the other hand focused on it formidable army and aimed to weaken athens by ravaging it countryside and devastating it economy
